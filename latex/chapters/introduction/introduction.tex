\section{Motivation} %%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%


% \todo{Explain why improving error messages is important for novice programmers.}


Understanding and interpreting error messages is a fundamental challenge for novice programmers.
Error messages serve as an essential form of feedback, helping programmers diagnose issues in their code.
However, standard error messages, particularly those generated by compilers and testing frameworks,
are often cryptic, overly technical, and difficult for beginners to decipher.\\
As a tutor, I frequently observe students struggling with these messages, unsure of what went wrong and how to fix their code.
Instead of guiding them toward a solution, poorly structured error messages often lead to frustration and trial-and-error debugging.
This hinders the learning process and increases the time required to grasp key programming concepts.\\
To address this issue, this thesis explores how improving error messages can enhance the learning experience for novice programmers.
By providing clearer, more structured feedback, we aim to help students understand their mistakes more effectively,
ultimately leading to a more efficient and engaging learning process.

\section{Research Problem and Objectives} %%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%


% \todo{Define the problem of unclear error messages and the goals of the thesis.}


The primary problem this thesis addresses is the difficulty novice programmers face when interpreting error messages.
Standard compiler and testing framework messages often assume prior knowledge, making them inaccessible to beginners.
This can lead to confusion, incorrect assumptions about the problem, and inefficient debugging strategies.
The objectives of this research are as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Analyze the impact of traditional error messages on the learning process.
    \item Develop a modified approach that enhances the clarity and usefulness of error messages.
    \item Evaluate whether improved error messages lead to faster learning and better debugging strategies among students.
\end{itemize}
To achieve these objectives, I have developed a test class that extends standard error reporting by adding a compact summary of test results.
This modification aims to provide clearer feedback, helping students identify and resolve issues more efficiently.

\section{Research Questions} %%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%


% \todo{List the key questions this thesis seeks to answer.}


This thesis seeks to answer the following key research questions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item How do traditional error messages impact the learning process of novice programmers?
    \item What specific issues do students encounter when interpreting standard error messages?
    \item Can modified error messages improve comprehension and debugging efficiency?
    \item How does structured feedback affect students' ability to correct their mistakes?
\end{itemize}
By addressing these questions, this research aims to provide insights into the effectiveness of improved error reporting and
its potential role in programming education.

\section{Research Structure} %%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%


% \todo{Briefly outline the chapters and their purpose.}


This thesis is structured as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Chapter 2: Related Work} -- Reviews existing research on challenges faced by novice programmers, the role of error messages in learning, and previous attempts at improving error feedback.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 3: Analysis of Previous Semester Data} -- Presents an analysis of past student submissions, including compilation success rates and test pass rates, to identify common issues.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 4: Problem Analysis} -- Examines specific problems with existing error messages, including real-world examples and gaps in the information they provide.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 5: Assertify --- A Library for Improving Error Messages} -- Introduces and explains the implementation of Assertify, a tool designed to enhance error feedback.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 6: Methodology} -- Describes the approach taken to evaluate the effectiveness of improved error messages.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 7: Evaluation and Results} -- Presents an analysis of the impact of modified error messages, including both quantitative and qualitative findings.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 8: Conclusion} -- Summarizes key findings, discusses their implications, and provides recommendations for future work.
    \item \textbf{Chapter 9: Discussion} -- Interprets the results, discusses their significance for programming education, and explores the broader implications of improved error messages.
\end{itemize}
This structure ensures a logical flow, moving from problem identification to solution development and evaluation, ultimately contributing to a better understanding of how error messages affect novice programmers.
