\begin{justify}
This chapter presents the design rationale and technical realisation of \emph{Assertify} and
\emph{Checkify}, a lightweight library intended to improve the pedagogical usefulness of test
feedback for novice programmers. The starting point is the requirement set derived in
\nameref{Problem Analysis}. Students need concise, consistently structured reports that make the
tested expression and the expected/actual mismatch explicit, while avoiding the fragmentation and
verbosity typical of baseline frameworks. The overall approach is \emph{non-invasive}: rather than
replacing existing test runners, Assertify/Checkify wrap established tooling and refine the reported
information before failing the test. \cite{becker,barik}
\end{justify}
