# Notes


- **Introduction:** motivates the problem, states objectives and research questions, and previews the thesis.
    * **Problem Statement and Objectives:** define the problem and list objectives.
    * **Research Questions:** state the guiding research questions.
    * **Thesis Structure:** summarise what each chapter contributes.

* **Background and Related Work:** summarise research on novice difficulties and prior approaches to improving feedback.
    * TODO: sections, other concrete literature besides barik, ..

* **Problem Analysis:** analyse baseline compiler/test output and derive requirements for improved reporting.
    * **Context and Assumptions:** specify setting, audience, and tooling assumptions.
    * **Observed Issues in Existing Compiler/Test Output:** identify recurring structural problems in baseline output.
    * **Derived Requirements and Design Implications:** derive requirements and connect them to design decisions.

* **Assertify (and Checkify): Design and Implementation:** present the design rationale and technical realisation of the artifact.
    * **Design Goals and Principles:** state guiding principles.
    * **Output Model: Representing Tests:** define the structured report/failure model.
    * **Mechanisms: Expression Capture and Value Extraction:** explain how expressions and values are captured/evaluated.
    * **Reporting: Formatting and Integration with the Test Pipeline:** describe formatting and integration with existing frameworks.

* **Results and Discussion:** present achieved properties and discuss insights, trade-offs, and limitations.
    * **Research Question Coverage and Requirement Satisfaction:** map results to RQs/requirements.
    * **Artifact Outcomes:** summarise supported features and produced outputs.
    * **Key Findings and Interpretations:** discuss main technical insights and implications.
    * **Strengths and Limitations:** state limitations and boundary conditions.

* **Conclusion:** summarise contributions and outline future work.
    * **Summary:** recap problem, approach, and contributions.
    * **Future Work:** list feasible extensions and next steps.
    * **Final Remarks:** provide the overall takeaway.
